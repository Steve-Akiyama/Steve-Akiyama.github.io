Who I am

Hi, I'm Steven Akiyama, a Computer Science student at Oregon State University passionate about AI and machine learning. I focus on applying these technologies to create innovative solutions, from AI-driven learning platforms to simulation optimization in reinforcement learning.

Currently, I lead TutorAI, a project utilizing large language models (LLMs) and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) for personalized student learning. I also recently led a reinforcement learning project to optimize plant growth, using Actor-Critic algorithms to enhance plant care strategies.

When I’m not coding, I’m in the kitchen, experimenting with new recipes. Lately, I’ve been perfecting a black pepper tofu dish that blends my love for flavor with precision (a skill I like to think translates into my coding too). I believe cooking, like programming, is all about experimenting, iterating, and achieving the perfect outcome.

I’m always eager to collaborate on projects that push the boundaries of AI and machine learning, and I’m looking for opportunities to apply my skills in real-world settings.
